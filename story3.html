The high-tech Hunger Games

Thabo went to KFC to buy some chicken. He wanted a simple meal, either the Streetwise menu or a family bucket. Just give him his chicken, and he's happy.

As he entered, he felt like he'd stepped into a whole new world. The restaurant was filled with screens everywhere! Ten big TVs showed more menu options than he had apps on his phone.

Thabo stood there, thinking, "Are they selling chicken or something else?" He approached a screen, squinting like he was reading a difficult book.

"Spicy Crunch Zinger Tower Triple Deluxe Extra Combo" and "Mega Wedges 3.0" stared back at him. Where was his humble Streetwise Two?

Thabo touched the screen, and it lit up with even more options. "Upgrade to Colonel's Gold Deluxe Edition meal for R10.99?" He thought, "Am I buying chicken or concert tickets?"

Everyone around him seemed to navigate the technology easily, customizing meals like pros. Meanwhile, Thabo sweated, scrolling through options like he was browsing online.

The screen asked him, "Are you sure you just want a Streetwise Two?" Thabo's frustration mounted. Finally, after what felt like forever, he made his selection.

His order popped up, R300! Thabo was stunned. He'd inadvertently ordered enough for a family reunion.

He looked around, wondering who had placed that massive order. Then it hit him ,it was him, trying to grab a simple Streetwise Two.

Thabo left KFC with a feast he didn't want and a newfound respect for those screens. As he took a bite of his crispy chicken, Thabo smiled and thought, “Next time, I'll know what I'm getting into.”

But one thing was certain, whether you're after the classics or looking to try something new, KFC is always ready to “Add Hope” to your day, one meal at a time.
